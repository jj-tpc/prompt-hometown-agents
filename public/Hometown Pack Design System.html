Outline colors

Sprout Lands uses two distinct outline colors depending on the subject — never plain black.

Default outline

#90625d — warm brown

Used for animals, tools, buildings, furniture, terrain edges. The general workhorse outline.

Character outline

#5c4e92 — signature purple

Reserved for human characters. This is the subtle visual marker Sprout Lands uses to make NPCs feel distinct from animals/items.

§ 02Character anatomy

Every NPC fits inside a 16×16 frame with the same proportions: oversized head (~7-9 px wide), tiny body (~6 px wide), short legs (~2 px). Eye spacing is fixed at 1-pixel-each with 2-pixel gap.

Row 0 — hair/hat top tufts
Two 3-pixel-wide caps separated by 4 pixels; or one continuous hat.
Rows 1–3 — head sides & hair
Head is 8 pixels wide, outlined. Hair sits in front; cream highlight on top.
Rows 4–7 — face
Cream fill #f3f4e7. Gray shadow #ddd5de in a 4-wide block. Eyes go on row 7 (cols 4 and 11 from the left).
Rows 8–12 — body / clothes
Body is 8 pixels wide at top, tapers to 6 at bottom. One accent stripe / button row in the middle.
Rows 13–15 — boots & feet
Two 2-pixel feet, with a 2-pixel gap between when idle.

Outline color quick check

  • Human NPC? → outline is #5c4e92 (Mage uses it).
  • Worker / commoner with brown outline? → that's fine too — Blacksmith, Merchant and Farmer use #90625d for a more grounded look. Pick one and stay consistent across all frames of that character.
§ 03Pixel-drawing rules
Do

Outline → mid → highlight, in that order

Every shape has 3 visible tones: the dark outline, the mid body color, and one bright highlight (cream #f3f4e7 or a 1-step-lighter cohort color).

Do

Anti-alias with palette colors, not opacity

No semi-transparency, no partial alpha. Soft edges come from picking an intermediate palette color (e.g. cream→tan→brown), not from rgba(…, 0.5).

Don't

No isolated single pixels for "shine"

One stray bright pixel inside an interior reads as noise, not shine. Cluster highlights in 2+ pixel shapes, ideally along the upper-left of a form.

Don't

No diagonal staircases longer than 4 px

Long mechanical staircases look harsh. Break them with a 1-pixel jog every 3-4 pixels (the same rule the original tilled-dirt edge uses).

Texture / speckle

Large interior areas (terrain tiles, walls, roofs) get scattered 1-pixel speckle of the next-darker palette step. Distribute by hand — no regular grid. Density: roughly 1 speckle per 32 pixels of interior.

§ 04Animation rules

Only characters and animals animate. Tilesets, items, furniture and buildings stay static — this matches the original Sprout Lands convention.

Walk cycle anatomy

The walk cycle is 4 frames at 220 ms / frame (≈ 4.5 fps): idle → walk-A → idle → walk-B. Only the bottom two rows change between frames.

Frame-by-frame deltas

  • Idle: both feet planted, 2-pixel gap between.
  • Walk-A: left foot 1px forward, right foot 1px back. Belly stripe shifts slightly to suggest motion.
  • Walk-B: mirror of A.
  • The torso is identical across all four frames. Save bytes (and consistency) by only redrawing the bottom rows.

4-direction layout

Sheet layout per NPC is one row of 6 frames: [front-idle, front-walk-A, front-walk-B, side-idle, side-walk-A, side-walk-B]. The "left" direction is the side sheet horizontally flipped at render time.

Normalization rule

Of the 256 possible 8-bit masks, only 47 produce visually distinct tiles. The reason: a diagonal neighbor's state only affects appearance if both of its adjacent edge neighbors are also biome. Otherwise the diagonal is hidden behind a convex corner. The lookup function normalizes a raw mask by clearing irrelevant diagonal bits:

function normalize(mask) {
  if (!((mask & N) && (mask & W))) mask &= ~NW;
  if (!((mask & N) && (mask & E))) mask &= ~NE;
  if (!((mask & S) && (mask & W))) mask &= ~SW;
  if (!((mask & S) && (mask & E))) mask &= ~SE;
  return mask;
}

Sheet layout

Each biome sheet is 192×64 (12 cols × 4 rows). Tiles are ordered by their normalized mask value, ascending. The 48th cell is intentionally blank (no biome at all = no tile needed).

Quadrant composition

Internally, each 16×16 tile is built from four 8×8 quadrants, each picked from 5 variants based on its 3 relevant neighbors (vertical edge, horizontal edge, diagonal corner):

  • Convex — both edge neighbors void → rounded outer corner
  • H-edge — only the horizontal neighbor is in → straight horizontal edge
  • V-edge — only the vertical neighbor is in → straight vertical edge
  • Full — all three in → interior with speckle
  • Concave — both edges in but diagonal out → inner-corner notch

This composition rule is why all 4 biomes generate from a single recipe — only the 5 palette keys (edge / mid / high / accent / shine) change.

§ 06Building dimensions

Multi-tile structures are always multiples of 16 in both dimensions, except the forge which extends to 47 px tall to fit a hanging sign. Anchor for placement is the bottom-left tile of the footprint.

forge.png · 32×47 well.png · 32×32 market-stall.png · 32×20 props.png · 64×16 (4 tiles)

Walls use stone gray with a 1-pixel light edge highlight. Roofs use cool cyan/teal (#8cbfc2) with diagonal speckle for a tiled-shingle feel. Wood frames use the cream/tan cohort with a 1-pixel #aa7959 mid-tone shadow.

§ 08Compatibility & license

This pack is designed as a drop-in expansion for the Sprout Lands asset set by Cup Nooble. Every color, outline, proportion and animation cadence matches the original — drop the PNGs alongside basic-character.png / grass.png / etc. and they will sit visually flush.
