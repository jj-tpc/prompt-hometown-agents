Terrain Tilesets

6 biome autotile sheets following the 47-tile blob bitmask system. Each sheet is 192×64 (12 × 4 tiles of 16×16). All four neighbor-edge combinations resolve to the right tile automatically.

How the autotile works

Each tile in a sheet covers one of the 47 unique neighbor configurations a cell can have. At runtime, compute the 8-bit mask of which neighbors share the same biome, normalize() it (clears irrelevant diagonal bits), and look up the tile index. The sheet is indexed by ascending mask value: tiles fill 12 columns × 4 rows, with the 48th cell unused.

Internally, each 16×16 tile is built from four 8×8 quadrants. Edge/corner quadrants share the same convex/edge/concave family across all six terrains — only the fullA / fullB interior patterns and the 5-color recipe (edge, mid, high, accent, shine) change. That's why the same shape language reads consistently from rough stone to polished tile.
